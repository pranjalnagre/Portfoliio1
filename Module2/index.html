#Computer Aided Design#

  • Computer-Aided Design (CAD) refers to the use of computer software to aid in the creation, modification, analysis, or optimization of a design. It's commonly used in various industries such as engineering, architecture, manufacturing, and product development.

  • CAD software allows designers and engineers to create precise 2D or 3D models of objects or systems. These models can range from simple geometric shapes to complex mechanical parts or architectural structures. CAD software typically provides tools for drafting, modeling, rendering, and simulation, allowing designers to visualize their designs and analyze their performance before they are physically built.
